Boxing

Information

"Boxing is a combat sport in which two people, usually wearing protective gloves, throw punches at each other for a predetermined set of time in a boxing ring." (Wikipedia). Fighter's goal is to land punch combinations to win by points or knockout.

Personal Experience

Began boxing at the age of 17 in a boxing gym located in Chicago called Scottsdale Park. I fought at the 156 pound weight class were I compete multiple times in Golden Gloves and several National tournaments.

Amateur vs Professional Boxing

Amateur Boxing

Also called Olympic Boxing. Fighter's usually fight with 12 or 10 oz. gloves. Headgears are wore by the boxers in order to protect head or brain injuries. Fights are short with three 4 minute rounds.

Professional Boxing

In Professional boxing, the average high-level fight is 12 3 minute rounds with a 1 minute break in between rounds. Fighters fight without headgear, which makes it a more dangerous compared to the Amateurs. Small gloves such as 8 or 10 oz. gloves are used. Fighters get paid per fight based on the amount of tickets sold or for the amount of ppv events sold. Other sources of income come from sponsorships, Boxing Federation etc.

Fighting Styles

There are three main fighting styles: the inside fighter, outside fighter, and the slugger.

  • An insider fighter is someone who fights in close combat. Typically fights in boxing shell and always is engaged close. Usually remains like this most of the fight.
  • An outside fighter(also known as the boxer) is someone who fights in faraway and close combat. Boxers combinations are set up, has good footwork,and good defense. After the combination is over, the fighter steps out to far combat (on the outside).
  • A slugger is someone rushes fowards nonstop,constantly throws power punches. This doesn't really involved technique and the main goal is to knockout the opponent.
